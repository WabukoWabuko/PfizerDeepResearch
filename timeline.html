Ventavia Timeline

Detailed chronology surrounding Brook Jackson, Ventavia Research Group, trial conduct allegations, regulatory escalation, and subsequent legal developments.

September 8, 2020

Brook Jackson begins employment as Regional Director at Ventavia Research Group. Within days, she allegedly identifies systemic concerns in documentation, participant handling, quality control, and trial management processes across multiple Texas sites.

September 15, 2020

First internal reporting occurs. Jackson reportedly informs management that participant charts were not properly quality-checked and some records had not been submitted to Pfizer or ICON. Questions around oversight, data integrity, and missing documentation emerge.

September 16, 2020

Evidence collection intensifies. Photographs allegedly document: biohazard concerns, HIPAA violations, publicly visible participant records, and randomization identifiers potentially affecting trial blinding. Emails and text messages are reportedly sent to supervisors including Marnie Fisher and Mercedes Livingston.

September 17, 2020

Critical internal discussions take place. Jackson allegedly raises concerns about FDA warning risks, protocol violations, and participant safety. Ventavia temporarily pauses enrollment. According to allegations, employees are instructed to describe the pause as a “bandwidth issue” instead of a compliance-related concern.

September 23–24, 2020

Detailed emails are sent regarding inconsistent data, scheduling deviations, missing laboratory samples, and informed consent discrepancies. Discussions reportedly include: temperature excursions, epinephrine documentation, and unblinding risks involving more than 1,000 participant records.

September 25, 2020

Jackson contacts the FDA hotline to report alleged violations. She is terminated the same day, with Ventavia reportedly stating she was “not a good fit.” Following termination, concerns are allegedly communicated to Pfizer personnel, including Dr. Arturo Alfaro.

2021 — Legal Action

A False Claims Act lawsuit is filed in federal court: United States ex rel. Brook Jackson v. Ventavia Research Group LLC et al. The lawsuit alleges: fraudulent trial conduct, protocol violations, retaliation, and regulatory failures.

March 31, 2023

The case is dismissed. The court rules that the allegations did not sufficiently demonstrate material fraud impacting government payment decisions or vaccine authorization outcomes. The ruling emphasizes: lack of evidence showing fabricated efficacy data or hidden safety failures material to authorization.
